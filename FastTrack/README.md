# Fast Track

These files/lessons are designed to get a student to do statistics/data science the "right way" as fast as possible. 
"Right way" is defined as a Bayesian causal model; the specific tools are jupyter notebooks and the bambi library.

## What you could learn

If you take the lessons in this folder seriously, and work to change your mind and perspective, then you could learn, in no particular order:

- How to determine if there is evidence that somethings causes something else--with or without an experiment.
- How to organize and visualize complicated and interconnected sets of hypotheses.
- methods to make pretty visuals to impress your boss or future employer--and the highfalutin language to go with them.

Hopefully, the first bullet point is the most exciting.

## Cavets

If the material in this FastTrack folder is your only exposure to the "right way" to do statistics, then you need to know that I am leaving out so, so much.

Specifically, here's the short version of what I'm leaving out:

- Data Collection
- Priors
- Likelihoods
- Posteriors
- Probability
- How to code/program
- Data Wrangling
- Formal treatment of confounders, colliders, and mediators
- Formal discussion of over and under fitting
- The pratical & mathematical relationship between the tool I chose (GLMs with bambi) and the litany of other valuable and valid methods.

Despite these many and numerous omissions, I still believe these materials are worth your time and attention. 
Doing things the "right way" poorly is often better than doing things the "wrong way" with finesse and grace.
